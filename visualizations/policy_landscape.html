How We Score Policies

Scoring Methodology

Policy strength is assessed across six key areas, with jurisdictions scored based on the presence, scope, and effectiveness of protections:

  • Hate Crime Laws: Does the jurisdiction have specific laws criminalising hate crimes against LGBTIQ+ people?
  • Vilification Laws: Are there civil/criminal prohibitions against public vilification on grounds of sexuality, gender identity, and intersex status?
  • Gender Recognition: How accessible is the legal gender change process? (Easy = self-determination, Moderate = some evidence required, Hard = surgery required)
  • Conversion Practices: Is there a comprehensive ban on conversion practices?
  • Discrimination Protections: Are there explicit protections in employment, housing, and services?
  • Religious Exemptions: How broad are exemptions allowing religious bodies to discriminate?

Scoring Categories

  • Strong (Green): 5-6 key protections present, limited religious exemptions, self-determination gender recognition
  • Moderate (Yellow): 3-4 key protections present, some gaps or broad exemptions
  • Weak (Red): 0-2 key protections, significant gaps, restrictive processes

Data Sources

All information is sourced from official government sources:

  • AustLII - Australian Legal Information Institute (public domain)
  • Federal Register of Legislation (public domain)
  • State/Territory legislation databases (public domain)
  • Parliament of Australia (public domain)
  • State/Territory parliament websites (public domain)

Limitations

This scoring provides a simplified overview and does not capture:

  • Enforcement effectiveness
  • Judicial interpretation of laws
  • Social and cultural factors affecting safety
  • Local government policies

For comprehensive analysis, consult official sources and legal experts.
