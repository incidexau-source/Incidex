## Overview

This comprehensive legal guide documents LGBTIQ+ discrimination and vilification protections across all Australian jurisdictions (Commonwealth + 8 states/territories) as at **31 December 2025**.

The guide is based strictly on **black-letter law** (statutory text) and official government sources, with no advocacy or speculation. It provides legally-accurate, plain-English information to power Incidex's "Know Your Rights" section.

## Key Findings

### Strongest Protections

1. **Tasmania** – Best in Australia
   - Full protections (sexual orientation incl. asexual, gender identity incl. non-binary, intersex variations)
   - Religious organisations can ONLY discriminate on religious belief
   - Comprehensive vilification protections

2. **ACT** – Very Strong
   - Full protections (sexuality, gender identity, sex characteristics)
   - Religious exemptions narrowed (2019, 2023)
   - Vilification protections

3. **Victoria** – Strong (with gap)
   - Full protections (sexual orientation, gender identity, sex characteristics)
   - Religious exemptions narrowed (2021)
   - ❌ NO vilification protections

### Weakest Protections

7. **WA** – Weak
   - Binary-only "gender history" definition
   - ❌ NO intersex protection
   - Very broad religious exemptions
   - ❌ NO vilification protections

8. **NSW** – Weakest
   - Outdated terminology (may not protect bisexual, non-binary people)
   - ❌ NO intersex protection
   - Small employer exemption (5 or fewer employees)
   - ALL private schools can discriminate
   - Very broad religious exemptions

### Vilification Protections

Only **5 jurisdictions** have LGBTIQ+ vilification protections:
- NSW (partial - complex framework)
- Queensland (comprehensive)
- Tasmania (comprehensive)
- ACT (comprehensive)
- NT (comprehensive)

**Commonwealth, Victoria, WA, and SA** have NO vilification protections for LGBTIQ+ people.

## Recent Reforms (2024-2025)

- **Queensland** (April 2024): Added sex characteristics, expanded gender identity definition
- **NSW** (July 2025): Gender recognition reforms (non-binary birth certificates) but discrimination law NOT updated
- **Victoria** (September 2025): Updated gender identity definition
- **NT** (October 2025): Amendments re-introduced narrower religious exemption

## Source Hierarchy

This guide follows a strict three-tier source hierarchy:

### Tier 1: Primary Legislation (Non-Negotiable)
- Commonwealth, state, and territory Anti-Discrimination Acts
- Official legislation portals (legislation.gov.au, legislation.nsw.gov.au, etc.)
- **Always prevails** in case of conflict

### Tier 2: Official Government Interpretation (Authoritative)
- Australian Human Rights Commission
- State/territory human rights and equal opportunity commissions
- Used for how Acts are applied in practice
- **Prevails over Tier 3** when interpreting legislation

### Tier 3: Secondary Sources (Cross-Check Only)
- Alastair Lawrie's "Quick Guide to Australian LGBTI Anti-Discrimination Laws" (2017)
- Equality Australia resources
- Law reform commission reports
- **Used for verification only**, never as primary authority

## Factsheet Template

The **Victoria factsheet** (`factsheets/victoria.md`) serves as a template for creating factsheets for other jurisdictions. Each factsheet should include:

1. **What's Protected** – Table of protected attributes
2. **Where Discrimination Is Unlawful** – Areas of coverage with examples
3. **Exemptions** – When discrimination may be legal
4. **Vilification** – Clear yes/no with explanation
5. **How to Report** – Commission, tribunal, and police contact details
6. **Where to Get Help** – Support services table
7. **Key Takeaway** – 2-3 sentence summary

All factsheets should:
- Use plain English (no legal jargon without explanation)
- Be accurate and non-advocacy
- Include specific examples
- Provide clear contact information
- Be 1-2 pages maximum
