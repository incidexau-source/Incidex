# National Comparison: LGBTIQ+ Discrimination & Vilification Laws in Australia

**Last updated: 19 February 2026**

This table compares LGBTIQ+ anti-discrimination and vilification protections across all Australian jurisdictions. It translates complex legal differences into plain English to help you understand your rights depending on where you live or work.

---

## Protected Attributes

| Jurisdiction | Sexual Orientation | Gender Identity | Intersex / Sex Characteristics | What It Means |
|--------------|-------------------|-----------------|-------------------------------|---------------|
| **Commonwealth** | ✓ | ⚠️ Unclear | ✓ Intersex Status | Basic national floor, but terminology is becoming outdated. |
| **NSW** | ⚠️ Gay/Lesbian only | ⚠️ Binary only | ❌ NOT protected | **Weakest in Australia.** Bi, Pan, Non-Binary, and Intersex people have no clear protection. |
| **Victoria** | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ Sex Characteristics + Medical Safeguards (2026) | Strong, modern protections for all identities. First state to legislate intersex medical safeguards. |
| **Queensland** | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ Sex Characteristics | **Gold Standard (New).** 2024 reforms made these the most inclusive definitions. |
| **WA** | ✓ | ⚠️ Binary only | ❌ NOT protected | Significant gaps for Non-Binary and Intersex people. Reforms are long overdue. |
| **SA** | ✓ | ⚠️ Unclear | ✓ Intersex Status | Moderate protections; terminology lags behind modern standards. |
| **Tasmania** | ✓ (incl. Asexual) | ✓ | ✓ Intersex Variations | **Gold Standard (Legacy).** Long-standing, highly inclusive protections. |
| **ACT** | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ Sex Characteristics | Strong, modern protections with clear inclusive language. |
| **NT** | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ Sex Characteristics | Strong protections following 2022/2023 reforms. |

### Key Issues
- **Terminology Matters:** NSW and WA use language from the 1980s. This "What It Means" is that if you are non-binary or intersex, you may not be able to make a legal complaint if you are fired or refused service.
- **The "Intersex Gap":** NSW and WA are the only jurisdictions that provide no explicit protection for people with variations in sex characteristics.

| Jurisdiction | Sexual Orientation | Gender Identity | Sex Characteristics | What It Means |
|--------------|-------------------|-----------------|---------------------|---------------|
| **Commonwealth** | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | No federal protection against LGBTIQ+ hate speech. |

| **Victoria** | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | No civil remedy for hate speech, despite government promises for years. |

| **SA** | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | No protections against LGBTIQ+ vilification. |
| **Tasmania** | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | Strong protections against conduct that "offends, insults, or humiliates." |

## Religious Exemptions
*Can religious schools or organisations legally discriminate?*

| Jurisdiction | Students Protected? | Teachers Protected? | What It Means |
|--------------|---------------------|--------------------|---------------|
| **Commonwealth** | ❌ NO | ❌ NO | Federal law allows broad discrimination in religious schools. |
| **NSW** | ❌ NO | ❌ NO | **Broadest exemptions.** ALL private schools (not just religious) can discriminate. |
| **Victoria** | ✅ YES | ✅ YES | Strong protection. Schools can only discriminate on religious belief, not identity. |
| **Queensland** | ✅ YES | ⚠️ Partial | Students are safe, but teachers are under "Don't Ask Don't Tell." |
| **WA** | ❌ NO | ❌ NO | Very broad. Schools can legally fire staff or expel students for being gay/trans. |
| **SA** | ⚠️ Unclear | ❌ NO | Complex laws that generally favor the religious organisation's right to discriminate. |
| **Tasmania** | ✅ YES | ✅ YES | **Best in practice.** Religion is almost never a "license to discriminate" against LGBTIQ+ people. |
| **ACT** | ✅ YES | ✅ YES | Strong, reformed protections since 2019/2023. |
| **NT** | ✅ YES | ✅ YES | Narrow exemptions, though October 2025 saw a minor step backward. |

## Overall Assessment (Rating)

### 🟢 GREEN: Strongest Protections
1. **Tasmania:** Still the gold standard. Inclusive, narrowest exemptions, and strong vilification laws.
2. **ACT:** Very close to Tasmania, with modern, clear legislation and strong student/teacher protections.
3. **Queensland:** Recent 2024 reforms have propelled QLD to the top tier for inclusivity and vilification laws.

### 🟡 AMBER: Moderate / Gaps
4. **Victoria:** Strong discrimination laws with historic intersex medical safeguards (2026), but let down by a total lack of vilification protections.
5. **NT:** Good modern laws, but recent 2025 amendments re-introducing exemptions are a concern.
6. **SA:** Protections exist but terminology is dated and religious exemptions remain broad.

### 🔴 RED: Weakest Protections
7. **Commonwealth:** Provides a basic floor but allows broad religious discrimination and has no vilification laws.
8. **WA:** Outdated binary language, no intersex protection, and broad religious licenses to discriminate.
9. **NSW:** **The worst in Australia.** Small business exemptions, no intersex protection, outdated binary language, and the broadest religious/private school exemptions in the country.

## Key Reforms (2024-2026)
- **VIC (Feb 2026):** Historic **Health Safeguards for People Born with Variations in Sex Characteristics Act 2025** passed (24-15). Victoria is the first Australian state to protect intersex children from unnecessary medical interventions. Establishes independent expert panel with lived experience representatives.
- **QLD (April 2024):** Massive update to include Sex Characteristics and inclusive Gender Identity.
- **VIC (Sept 2025):** Updated definitions to ensure Non-Binary people are clearly protected.
- **NT (Oct 2025):** Re-introduced some religious exemptions, slightly weakening the 2023 gains.
- **WA (Pending):** Reforms promised for years remain stalled as of February 2026.
