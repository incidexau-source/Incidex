# Know Your Rights: LGBTIQ+ Discrimination & Vilification (Tasmania)

**Last updated: 31 December 2025**

---

## Overall Rating: 🟢 GREEN

Tasmania is widely considered to have the **best LGBTIQ+ anti-discrimination protections in Australia**. Sexual orientation (including asexual), gender identity (including non-binary), and intersex variations are all protected. Most importantly, Tasmania has the strongest protections in religious schools and organisations—they can only discriminate based on religious belief, not LGBTIQ+ status.

---

## What's Protected

### Sexual Orientation: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against because you are gay, lesbian, bisexual, or asexual.

**Real-world examples:**
- Your employer cannot fire you for being gay, lesbian, bisexual, or asexual.
- A landlord cannot refuse to rent to you because you're in a same-sex relationship.
- A hospital cannot deny you treatment based on sexual orientation.
- **A religious school cannot refuse to hire you as a teacher just because you're gay.**
- A café cannot refuse to serve you because you're in a same-sex relationship.

**Limitations:** None. Tasmania explicitly includes asexual people.

### Gender Identity: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against based on your gender identity, including if you are transgender, non-binary, genderfluid, or agender.

**Real-world examples:**
- Your employer must use your correct name and pronouns.
- You cannot be denied employment because you are transitioning or non-binary.
- A landlord cannot refuse housing because you are trans or non-binary.
- Schools cannot prevent you from using facilities matching your gender identity.
- **Religious schools cannot refuse to hire you as a teacher or expel you as a student for being trans or non-binary.**

**Limitations:** None. Tasmania has one of the most inclusive gender identity definitions in Australia (updated in 2019).

### Intersex Status: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against based on intersex variations of sex characteristics (chromosomes, hormones, anatomy).

**Real-world examples:**
- You cannot be denied medical care based on being intersex.
- An employer cannot ask invasive questions about your body.
- You cannot be excluded from school activities because of sex characteristics.
- **A religious school cannot refuse to hire you just because you're intersex.**

**Limitations:** None. Tasmania was the first state to update its law to the modern "intersex variations of sex characteristics" terminology.

---

## Where You're Protected

### EMPLOYMENT ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Being rejected for a job because of sexual orientation/gender identity/intersex variations.
- Being paid less than colleagues because you're LGBTIQ+.
- Being harassed by coworkers or managers.
- Being forced to hide your identity at work.
- Being fired because you transition or come out.
- **Religious organisations** refusing to hire you just because you're LGBTIQ+.

**Not protected from:**
- Performance-based discipline (if you're underperforming for legitimate work reasons).

### EDUCATION ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Schools refusing to enroll your child because you're a same-sex couple.
- Universities discriminating in admissions.
- Teachers intentionally using wrong pronouns.
- Being excluded from school events, sports, or facilities.
- **Religious schools** expelling or refusing enrollment to students for being LGBTIQ+.

### HOUSING ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Landlords refusing to rent because you're in a same-sex relationship.
- Being evicted because of your gender identity or intersex variations.
- Being denied mortgage approval because you're LGBTIQ+.

### GOODS & SERVICES ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Being refused service at a café or restaurant.
- A doctor refusing to treat you.
- **Religious service providers** (like welfare agencies) refusing to serve you.

## Exemptions: When Discrimination May Be Legal

### Religious Organisations – THE NARROWEST IN AUSTRALIA

**What this means:** Tasmania has the strongest protections against religious discrimination in Australia. Religious organisations can **ONLY** discriminate based on religious belief, NOT on LGBTIQ+ status.

**They CAN:**
- Religious schools can require staff and students to uphold religious beliefs.
- Religious organisations can make decisions based on whether someone shares their religious faith.

**They CANNOT:**
- Religious schools **cannot** refuse to hire or enroll someone just for being gay, lesbian, bisexual, asexual, trans, or intersex.
- Religious organisations **cannot** refuse to provide services to someone because they are LGBTIQ+.

**Real example:** A Catholic school can require teachers to attend Mass, but cannot refuse to hire a teacher simply because they are gay. The school can only make decisions based on **religious belief**, not LGBTIQ+ status. This is the strongest protection in the country.

**If you face discrimination:** Tasmania is the "Gold Standard." If any organisation claims they can legally discriminate against you for being LGBTIQ+, they are likely wrong. Seek legal advice from **Equal Opportunity Tasmania**.

## Vilification Laws

**Does Tasmania have vilification laws for LGBTIQ+ people?**  
✅ YES

Tasmania has comprehensive vilification protections. It is illegal to publicly incite hatred, serious contempt, or severe ridicule against someone because they are LGBTIQ+. This covers sexual orientation, gender identity, and intersex variations.

---

## How to Report Discrimination

You can lodge a complaint with **Equal Opportunity Tasmania**.

- **Website:** [equalopportunity.tas.gov.au](https://www.equalopportunity.tas.gov.au)
- **Phone:** 1300 305 062
- **Time Limit:** You must lodge your complaint within **12 months**.

## Key Takeaway

Tasmania is the gold standard for LGBTIQ+ rights in Australia. Its laws are simple, inclusive, and provide the same protections in religious organisations as they do everywhere else. If you are LGBTIQ+ in Tasmania, you have the most powerful legal shield in the country.
