# Know Your Rights: LGBTIQ+ Discrimination & Vilification (Western Australia)

**Last updated: 31 December 2025**

---

## Overall Rating: 🔴 RED

WA has weak LGBTIQ+ protections. The law uses binary terminology that does not clearly protect non-binary or gender diverse people. Intersex status is **NOT protected**. While the Law Reform Commission recommended major changes in 2022 and the Government committed to them, no legislation has been passed as of December 2025.

---

## What's Protected

### Sexual Orientation: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against because you are heterosexual, gay, lesbian, or bisexual.

**Real-world examples:**
- Your employer cannot fire you for being gay, lesbian, or bisexual.
- A landlord cannot refuse to rent to you because you're in a same-sex relationship.
- A hospital cannot deny you treatment based on sexual orientation.
- A café cannot refuse to serve you because you're in a same-sex relationship.

**Limitations:** Asexual people are not explicitly mentioned and may lack protection. Very broad religious exemptions mean religious organisations can still legally discriminate.

### Gender Identity: ⚠️ Limited

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against if you are transgender with a binary identity (male or female). Non-binary people are **NOT clearly protected**.

**Real-world examples:**
- Your employer must use your correct name and pronouns if you're a trans man or trans woman.
- You cannot be denied employment because you are transitioning from male to female or female to male.
- A landlord cannot refuse housing because you are a trans man or trans woman.

**Limitations:** WA law uses the binary term "gender history" and "opposite sex," which leaves non-binary and gender diverse people in a legal gray area. Employers can also discriminate to enforce "dress and appearance standards" against trans people. Reforms were promised in 2022 but have not happened.

### Intersex Status / Sex Characteristics: ❌ Not Protected

**What this means:** Intersex people and people with variations in sex characteristics are **NOT protected** in WA.

**Real-world examples:**
- An employer **CAN** ask invasive questions about your body if you're intersex.
- A school **CAN** exclude you from activities because of sex characteristics.
- A gym **CAN** refuse you membership because you are intersex.

**Limitations:** This is a major gap. The Law Reform Commission recommended adding this protection in 2022, but the law remains unchanged as of December 2025.

---

## Where You're Protected

### EMPLOYMENT ⚠️
**What you're protected from:**
- Being rejected for a job because you're gay/lesbian/bisexual or trans (binary only).
- Being paid less than colleagues because you're LGBTIQ+.
- Being harassed by coworkers or managers.

**Not protected from:**
- **Discrimination by religious organisations** (very broad exemptions).
- **Dress and appearance standards** (employers can legally discriminate to enforce these).
- Discrimination if you are asexual, non-binary, or intersex.

### EDUCATION ⚠️
**What you're protected from:**
- Public schools refusing to enroll your child because you're a same-sex couple.
- Public universities discriminating in admissions.

**Not protected from:**
- **Religious schools** can legally discriminate against LGBTIQ+ students and staff (very broad exemptions).

### HOUSING ⚠️
**What you're protected from:**
- Landlords refusing to rent because you're gay/lesbian/bisexual or trans (binary).
- Being evicted because of your sexual orientation or gender identity.

### GOODS & SERVICES ⚠️
**What you're protected from:**
- Being refused service at a café or restaurant (if you're gay/lesbian/bisexual or binary trans).
- A public hospital refusing to treat you.

**Not protected from:**
- Discrimination by religious organisations (very broad exemptions).

---

## Exemptions: When Discrimination May Be Legal

### Religious Organisations – VERY BROAD

**What this means:** WA has some of the broadest religious exemptions in Australia. Religious organisations can discriminate against LGBTIQ+ people in almost all circumstances.

**They CAN:**
- Religious schools can refuse to hire LGBTIQ+ teachers.
- Religious schools can expel or refuse to enroll LGBTIQ+ students.
- Religious organisations can refuse to provide services to LGBTIQ+ people.
- Religious hospitals can refuse to hire LGBTIQ+ staff.

**Real example:** A Catholic school in Perth can legally refuse to hire a gay teacher or expel a trans student. A religious hospital can refuse to employ a lesbian nurse.

**If you face discrimination:** WA law is currently very weak. While the Law Reform Commission recommended narrowing these exemptions in 2022, the changes have not been made. Seek legal advice from the **Equal Opportunity Commission WA**.

---

## Vilification Laws

**Does WA have vilification laws for LGBTIQ+ people?**  
❌ NO

WA only has vilification laws for **race**. There are no protections against hate speech or conduct that incites hatred against the LGBTIQ+ community.

---

## How to Report Discrimination

You can lodge a complaint with the **Equal Opportunity Commission of Western Australia**.

- **Website:** [eoc.wa.gov.au](https://www.eoc.wa.gov.au)
- **Phone:** 1800 198 149
- **Time Limit:** You must lodge your complaint within **12 months**.

## Key Takeaway

WA is stuck with 40-year-old laws that have failed to keep pace with the rest of the country. Despite a comprehensive report in 2022 recommending major improvements, the WA Government has been slow to act. Non-binary and intersex people remain completely or partially invisible in WA's discrimination law.
