# Know Your Rights: LGBTIQ+ Discrimination & Vilification (New South Wales)

**Last updated: 31 December 2025**

---

## Overall Rating: 🔴 RED

NSW has the weakest LGBTIQ+ protections in Australia. The law uses outdated terminology that may not protect bisexual or non-binary people. Intersex people are **NOT protected**. NSW is the only jurisdiction with a small employer exemption for LGBTIQ+ discrimination. **ALL private schools** (not just religious ones) can discriminate. Religious exemptions are very broad.

---

## What's Protected

### Sexual Orientation: ⚠️ Limited

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against because you are gay or lesbian. However, bisexual and asexual people may not be clearly protected because the law uses the outdated term "homosexuality" (defined as male or female homosexuality).

**Real-world examples:**
- Your employer (with more than 5 staff) cannot fire you for being gay or lesbian.
- A landlord cannot refuse to rent to you because you're in a same-sex relationship.
- A public hospital cannot deny you treatment based on being gay or lesbian.
- A café cannot refuse to serve you because you're gay or lesbian.

**Limitations:** Small employers (5 or fewer staff) can legally discriminate. ALL private schools can discriminate. Bisexual and asexual people are not explicitly mentioned and may lack protection.

### Gender Identity: ⚠️ Limited

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against if you are transgender with a binary identity (male or female). Non-binary people are not clearly protected.

**Real-world examples:**
- Your employer (with more than 5 staff) must use your correct name and pronouns if you're a trans man or trans woman.
- You cannot be denied employment because you are transitioning from male to female or female to male.
- A landlord cannot refuse housing because you are a trans man or trans woman.

**Limitations:** The law uses binary language ("opposite sex") which does not clearly protect non-binary or gender diverse people. While you can get a non-binary birth certificate in NSW (since July 2025), the discrimination law has not been updated to match. Small employers and all private schools can legally discriminate.

### Intersex Status / Sex Characteristics: ❌ Not Protected

**What this means:** Intersex people and people with variations in sex characteristics are **NOT protected** in NSW.

**Real-world examples:**
- An employer **CAN** ask invasive questions about your body if you're intersex.
- A school **CAN** exclude you from activities because of sex characteristics.
- A gym **CAN** refuse you membership because you are intersex.

**Limitations:** This is a major gap in NSW law. As of December 2025, there is no protection for intersex status or sex characteristics.

## Where You're Protected

### EMPLOYMENT ⚠️
**What you're protected from:**
- Being rejected for a job because you're gay/lesbian or trans (binary only) - **BUT only if the employer has more than 5 staff**.
- Being paid less than colleagues because you're gay/lesbian or trans.
- Being harassed by coworkers or managers.

**Not protected from:**
- **Discrimination by small employers (5 or fewer employees)** - they can legally discriminate against you.
- **Discrimination by ALL private schools** (not just religious ones).
- Discrimination by religious organisations (very broad exemptions).

### EDUCATION ⚠️
**What you're protected from:**
- Public schools refusing to enroll your child because you're a same-sex couple.
- Public universities discriminating in admissions.

**Not protected from:**
- **ALL private schools** (even non-religious ones) can discriminate against LGBTIQ+ students and staff. NSW is the only state that allows this.

### HOUSING ⚠️
**What you're protected from:**
- Landlords refusing to rent because you're gay/lesbian or trans (binary).
- Being evicted because of your sexual orientation or gender identity.

### GOODS & SERVICES ⚠️
**What you're protected from:**
- Being refused service at a café or restaurant (if you're gay/lesbian or binary trans).
- A public hospital refusing to treat you.

**Not protected from:**
- Discrimination by religious organisations (very broad exemptions).

## Exemptions: When Discrimination May Be Legal

### Religious Organisations & Private Schools – VERY BROAD

**What this means:** NSW has the broadest exemptions in Australia. Religious organisations can discriminate against LGBTIQ+ people in almost all circumstances. Additionally, **ALL private schools** (even non-religious ones) can discriminate.

**They CAN:**
- Religious schools can refuse to hire LGBTIQ+ teachers.
- Religious schools can expel LGBTIQ+ students.
- **ALL private schools** (even non-religious ones) can refuse to hire or enroll LGBTIQ+ people.
- Religious hospitals and welfare agencies can refuse to hire LGBTIQ+ staff.

**Small Employer Exemption:**
- If a business has **5 or fewer employees**, they can legally refuse to hire you or fire you because you are gay, lesbian, or trans.

**Real example:** A non-religious private school in Sydney can legally refuse to enroll a student because they have two dads. A small café with 4 staff can legally refuse to hire a trans woman.

**If you face discrimination:** NSW has the weakest protections in Australia. If you face discrimination, seek legal advice from **Anti-Discrimination NSW**, but be aware that the exemptions are very broad. You may have better luck under Commonwealth law, though it also has gaps.

## Vilification Laws

**Does NSW have vilification laws for LGBTIQ+ people?**  
⚠️ PARTIAL

- **Civil:** You can lodge a complaint for vilification if you are gay, lesbian, or a trans person with a binary identity.
- **Criminal:** It is a crime to incite violence against someone based on their sexual orientation or gender identity (this part is broader and includes bisexual and non-binary people).
- **Intersex:** There is **no civil protection** against intersex vilification.

---

## How to Report Discrimination

You can lodge a complaint with **Anti-Discrimination NSW**.

- **Website:** [antidiscrimination.nsw.gov.au](https://www.antidiscrimination.nsw.gov.au)
- **Phone:** 1800 670 812
- **Time Limit:** You must lodge your complaint within **12 months**.

## Key Takeaway

NSW is significantly behind the rest of Australia. It is the only state where small businesses can legally discriminate against most LGBTIQ+ people, and the only state where **every** private school has a "license to discriminate." Reforms are urgently needed to bring NSW Law into the 21st century.
