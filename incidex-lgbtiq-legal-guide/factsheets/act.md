# Know Your Rights: LGBTIQ+ Discrimination & Vilification (Australian Capital Territory)

**Last updated: 31 December 2025**

---

## Overall Rating: 🟢 GREEN

The ACT has very strong LGBTIQ+ protections. Sexuality (including bisexual and intersex), gender identity (including non-binary), and sex characteristics are all comprehensively protected. Religious organisations and schools have significantly narrowed exemptions—they can only discriminate based on religious belief, not LGBTIQ+ status.

---

## What's Protected

### Sexual Orientation: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against because you are gay, lesbian, or bisexual. The ACT uniquely includes "being intersex" under its definition of sexuality as well.

**Real-world examples:**
- Your employer cannot fire you for being gay, lesbian, or bisexual.
- A landlord cannot refuse to rent to you because you're in a same-sex relationship.
- A hospital cannot deny you treatment based on sexual orientation.
- **A religious school cannot refuse to hire you as a teacher just because you're gay.**
- A café cannot refuse to serve you because you're in a same-sex relationship.

**Limitations:** Asexual people are not explicitly mentioned in the sexuality definition, though they may still be protected.

### Gender Identity: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against based on your gender identity, including if you are transgender, non-binary, genderfluid, or agender.

**Real-world examples:**
- Your employer must use your correct name and pronouns.
- You cannot be denied employment because you are transitioning or non-binary.
- A landlord cannot refuse housing because you are trans or non-binary.
- Schools cannot prevent you from using facilities matching your gender identity.
- **A religious school cannot refuse to hire you or expel you for being trans or non-binary.**

**Limitations:** None. The ACT has a modern and inclusive gender identity definition.

### Sex Characteristics: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against based on physical sex characteristics (chromosomes, hormones, anatomy). This includes intersex variations.

**Real-world examples:**
- You cannot be denied medical care based on being intersex.
- An employer cannot ask invasive questions about your body.
- You cannot be excluded from school activities because of sex characteristics.
- **A religious school cannot refuse to hire you just because you're intersex.**

**Limitations:** None. The ACT replaced "intersex status" with more accurate "sex characteristics" terminology in 2020.

---

## Where You're Protected

### EMPLOYMENT ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Being rejected for a job because of sexual orientation/gender identity/sex characteristics.
- Being paid less than colleagues because you're LGBTIQ+.
- Being harassed by coworkers or managers.
- Being forced to hide your identity at work.
- Being fired because you transition or come out.
- **Religious organisations** refusing to hire you just because you're LGBTIQ+ (they can only discriminate based on religious belief).

**Not protected from:**
- Performance-based discipline (if you're underperforming for legitimate work reasons).

### EDUCATION ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Schools refusing to enroll your child because you're a same-sex couple.
- Universities discriminating in admissions.
- Teachers intentionally using wrong pronouns.
- Being excluded from school events, sports, or facilities.
- **Religious schools** expelling or refusing enrollment to students for being LGBTIQ+.

### HOUSING ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Landlords refusing to rent because you're in a same-sex relationship.
- Being evicted because of your gender identity or sex characteristics.

### GOODS & SERVICES ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Being refused service at a café or restaurant.
- A doctor refusing to treat you.
- **Religious organisations** (like welfare agencies or hospitals) refusing to serve you just because you're LGBTIQ+.

## Exemptions: When Discrimination May Be Legal

### Religious Organisations – NARROW REFORMED EXEMPTIONS

**What this means:** The ACT has significantly narrowed religious exemptions. Organisations can no longer discriminate against you just for being LGBTIQ+.

**They CAN:**
- Religious schools can require staff and students to uphold religious beliefs.
- Religious organisations can make decisions based on whether someone shares their religious faith.

**They CANNOT:**
- Religious schools **cannot** refuse to hire someone just for being gay, lesbian, bisexual, trans, or intersex.
- Religious schools **cannot** expel students for being LGBTIQ+.
- Religious organisations **cannot** refuse services to LGBTIQ+ people.

**Real example:** A religious school in Canberra can require teachers to uphold certain religious values, but cannot refuse to hire a teacher simply because they are gay or transgender. Decisions must be based on **religious belief**, not LGBTIQ+ status.

**If you face discrimination:** The ACT has some of the strongest protections in Australia. If any organisation claims they can legally discriminate against you for being LGBTIQ+, seek advice from the **ACT Human Rights Commission**.

## Vilification Laws

**Does the ACT have vilification laws for LGBTIQ+ people?**  
✅ YES

The ACT has civil vilification laws. It is illegal to publicly incite hatred, serious contempt, or severe ridicule against someone because of their sexuality (which includes being intersex) or gender identity.

---

## How to Report Discrimination

You can lodge a complaint with the **ACT Human Rights Commission**.

- **Website:** [hrc.act.gov.au](https://hrc.act.gov.au)
- **Phone:** (02) 6205 2222
- **Time Limit:** You must lodge your complaint within **12 months**.

## Key Takeaway

The ACT is a very safe jurisdiction for the LGBTIQ+ community. Its laws are modern, inclusive, and provide strong protection even within religious schools and hospitals. Along with Tasmania, it sets the standard for how Australian discrimination law should look.
