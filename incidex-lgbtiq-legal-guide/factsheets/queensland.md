# Know Your Rights: LGBTIQ+ Discrimination & Vilification (Queensland)

**Last updated: 17 March 2026**

---

## Overall Rating: 🟡 AMBER

Queensland has good protections following major reforms in April 2024. Sexual orientation, gender identity (including non-binary), and sex characteristics are all protected. Students in religious schools are fully protected. Vilification protections are comprehensive. However, religious schools can still discriminate against LGBTIQ+ staff under "genuine occupational requirements," which prevents it from being rated Green.

---

## What's Protected

### Sexual Orientation: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against based on your sexual orientation, including if you are gay, lesbian, bisexual, or any other orientation.

**Real-world examples:**
- Your employer cannot fire you for being gay, lesbian, or bisexual.
- A landlord cannot refuse to rent to you because you're in a same-sex relationship.
- A hospital cannot deny you treatment based on sexual orientation.
- A school cannot expel your child for having same-sex parents.
- A café cannot refuse to serve you because you're in a same-sex relationship.

**Limitations:** Queensland uses the term "lawful sexual activity" rather than "sexual orientation," but this provides comprehensive protection.

### Gender Identity: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against based on your gender identity, including if you are transgender, non-binary, genderfluid, or agender.

**Real-world examples:**
- Your employer must use your correct name and pronouns.
- You cannot be denied employment because you are transitioning or non-binary.
- A landlord cannot refuse housing because you are trans or non-binary.
- Schools cannot prevent you from using facilities matching your gender identity.
- Religious schools **cannot** expel students for being trans or non-binary.

**Limitations:** Queensland's definition was expanded in April 2024 to explicitly include non-binary and gender diverse people. For staff in religious schools, a "Don't Ask Don't Tell" policy applies—they cannot ask for your identity, but can discriminate if it becomes known and reaches "genuine occupational requirements."

### Sex Characteristics: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against based on physical sex characteristics (chromosomes, hormones, anatomy). This includes intersex status.

**Real-world examples:**
- You cannot be denied medical care based on being intersex.
- An employer cannot ask invasive questions about your body.
- You cannot be excluded from school activities because of sex characteristics.
- A gym cannot refuse you membership because you are intersex.

**Limitations:** None. Queensland added "sex characteristics" as a brand new protection in April 2024.

## Where You're Protected

### EMPLOYMENT ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Being rejected for a job because of sexual orientation/gender identity/sex characteristics.
- Being paid less than colleagues because you're LGBTIQ+.
- Being harassed by coworkers or managers.
- Being fired because you transition or come out.

**Not protected from:**
- Performance-based discipline (if you're underperforming for legitimate work reasons).
- **Religious school staff** can be discriminated against if they reach "genuine occupational requirements."

### EDUCATION ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Schools refusing to enroll your child because you're a same-sex couple.
- Universities discriminating in admissions.
- Teachers intentionally using wrong pronouns.
- Being excluded from school events, sports, or facilities.
- **Religious schools** expelling or refusing enrollment to students for being LGBTIQ+.

### HOUSING ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Landlords refusing to rent because you're in a same-sex relationship.
- Being evicted because of your gender identity or sex characteristics.
- Being denied mortgage approval because you're LGBTIQ+.

### GOODS & SERVICES ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Being refused service at a café or restaurant.
- A doctor refusing to treat you.
- Hotels refusing to book rooms for same-sex couples.

## Exemptions: When Discrimination May Be Legal

### Religious Organisations – STAFF ONLY

**What this means:** Queensland protects LGBTIQ+ students strongly, but has limited exemptions for staff in religious organisations.

**They CAN:**
- Religious schools can discriminate against LGBTIQ+ staff where it breaches "genuine occupational requirements" of the position.

**They CANNOT:**
- Religious schools **cannot** expel or refuse to enroll LGBTIQ+ students.
- Religious schools **cannot** ask for information about a staff member's sexual orientation or gender identity (they operate a "Don't Ask Don't Tell" policy).
- Religious organisations do not have a general right to discriminate—only where it's a genuine requirement of the job.

**Real example:** A religious school cannot expel a student for being gay. However, if a teacher at the school publicly comes out, the school might try to argue they can no longer meet the "genuine occupational requirements" of teaching in a religious context.

**If you face discrimination:** Queensland has some of the best student protections in the country. If you are a staff member, the law is more complex. Seek legal advice from the **Queensland Human Rights Commission (QHRC)**.

## Gender-Affirming Care for Minors

**Does Queensland allow gender-affirming medical care for trans/gender diverse minors?**
⚠️ RESTRICTED — BAN IN EFFECT UNTIL 2031

Queensland has a **regulatory ban** on gender-affirming medical care (puberty blockers and hormone therapy) for minors who are new patients. The ban was originally introduced in 2023 as a temporary measure and was extended to **31 December 2031** by regulation in December 2025, following a review by Dr Cate Vine (the Vine Review, 2024).

**What this means:**
- New patients under 18 **cannot** commence puberty blockers or hormone therapy.
- Existing patients who were already receiving treatment **may** continue with ministerial approval.
- Surgical procedures (rare in minors) were not the subject of the ban.

**Legal challenge underway:** LGBTI Legal Service Inc. has filed a legal challenge in QCAT arguing the ban is discriminatory and unlawful. This challenge is ongoing as of March 2026.

**If you are a trans or gender diverse young person in Queensland:** Access peer support through **QLife** (1800 184 527) or **Brisbane Youth Service**. For legal information, contact **LGBTI Legal Service** (07 3124 7160).

## Vilification Laws

**Does Queensland have vilification laws for LGBTIQ+ people?**
✅ YES

Queensland has some of the best vilification protections in Australia. It is illegal to publicly incite hatred, serious contempt, or severe ridicule against someone because they are LGBTIQ+. This includes:
- **Civil:** You can lodge a complaint and get a remedy (like an apology or compensation).
- **Criminal:** Serious cases involving threats of violence are a crime.

---

## How to Report Discrimination

You can lodge a complaint with the **Queensland Human Rights Commission (QHRC)**.

- **Website:** [qhrc.qld.gov.au](https://www.qhrc.qld.gov.au)
- **Phone:** 1300 130 670
- **LGBTIQ+ Liaison:** [lgbtiq@qhrc.qld.gov.au](mailto:lgbtiq@qhrc.qld.gov.au)
- **Time Limit:** You must lodge your complaint within **12 months**.

## Key Takeaway

Queensland is currently one of the safest places for LGBTIQ+ students and residents. Following major 2024 reforms, the law is modern and inclusive, particularly for non-binary and intersex people. The addition of full vilification protections makes Queensland a leader in Australia for LGBTIQ+ rights.
