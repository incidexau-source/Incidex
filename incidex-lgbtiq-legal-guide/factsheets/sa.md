# Know Your Rights: LGBTIQ+ Discrimination & Vilification (South Australia)

**Last updated: 31 December 2025**

---

## Overall Rating: 🟡 AMBER

South Australia has moderate protections. While sexual orientation, gender identity, and intersex status are all protected, broad religious exemptions allow religious organisations to discriminate in most circumstances. The protection for LGBTIQ+ students in religious schools is particularly unclear, and there are no vilification protections.

---

## What's Protected

### Sexual Orientation: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against because you are heterosexual, gay, lesbian, or bisexual.

**Real-world examples:**
- Your employer cannot fire you for being gay, lesbian, or bisexual.
- A landlord cannot refuse to rent to you because you're in a same-sex relationship.
- A hospital cannot deny you treatment based on sexual orientation.
- A café cannot refuse to serve you because you're in a same-sex relationship.

**Limitations:** Asexual people are not explicitly protected. Broad religious exemptions mean religious organisations can still legally discriminate in many circumstances.

### Gender Identity: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against based on your gender identity, including if you are transgender, non-binary, genderfluid, or agender.

**Real-world examples:**
- Your employer must use your correct name and pronouns.
- You cannot be denied employment because you are transitioning or non-binary.
- A landlord cannot refuse housing because you are trans or non-binary.

**Limitations:** Protection for non-binary people is not explicitly clear. Employers are also allowed to discriminate to enforce "standards of appearance and dress" against trans people. Religious schools have broad exemptions.

### Intersex Status: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against based on intersex status (chromosomes, hormones, anatomy).

**Real-world examples:**
- You cannot be denied medical care based on being intersex.
- An employer cannot ask invasive questions about your body.
- You cannot be excluded from school activities because of intersex status.
- A gym cannot refuse you membership because you are intersex.

**Limitations:** Protection was added in 2016. However, broad religious exemptions may still apply.

---

## Where You're Protected

### EMPLOYMENT ⚠️
**What you're protected from:**
- Being rejected for a job because of sexual orientation/gender identity/intersex status.
- Being paid less than colleagues because you're LGBTIQ+.
- Being harassed by coworkers or managers.
- Being fired because you transition or come out.

**Not protected from:**
- **Discrimination by religious organisations** (broad exemptions).
- **Dress and appearance standards** (employers can legally discriminate to enforce these against trans people).
- **Religious schools** can likely discriminate against LGBTIQ+ teachers (though they must follow certain procedural rules).

### EDUCATION ⚠️
**What you're protected from:**
- Public schools refusing to enroll your child because you're a same-sex couple.
- Public universities discriminating in admissions.

**Not protected from:**
- **Religious schools** may be able to discriminate against LGBTIQ+ students. The law is very complex and unclear on this point, but broad exemptions usually favor the school.

### HOUSING ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Landlords refusing to rent because you're in a same-sex relationship.
- Being evicted because of your gender identity or intersex status.
- Real estate agents refusing to show you properties.

### GOODS & SERVICES ⚠️
**What you're protected from:**
- Being refused service at a café or restaurant.
- A public hospital refusing to treat you.
- Hotels refusing to book rooms for same-sex couples.

**Not protected from:**
- Discrimination by religious organisations (broad exemptions).

---

## Exemptions: When Discrimination May Be Legal

### Religious Organisations – BROAD EXEMPTIONS

**What this means:** SA has broad religious exemptions. Religious organisations can discriminate against LGBTIQ+ people in many situations.

**They CAN:**
- Religious schools can likely discriminate against LGBTIQ+ students (the law is very unclear, but exemptions are broad).
- Religious schools can discriminate against LGBTIQ+ teachers (but they **must** have a written policy provided to employees and the public).
- Religious organisations can discriminate in how they provide services.

**They MUST:**
- If a religious school wants to discriminate against staff, they **must** provide a written policy to employees, students, parents, and the public on request.

**Real example:** A religious school in Adelaide can likely refuse to enroll a student for being gay, or fire a teacher for being trans, provided they have a written policy that says they will do so.

**If you face discrimination:** SA's protections are some of the most confusing in Australia, especially regarding students in religious schools. If you face discrimination, seek legal advice from the **Equal Opportunity Commission SA**.

---

## Vilification Laws

**Does SA have vilification laws for LGBTIQ+ people?**  
❌ NO

SA only has vilification laws for **race**. There are no laws prohibiting anti-LGBTIQ+ hate speech or conduct that incites hatred.

---

## How to Report Discrimination

You can lodge a complaint with the **Equal Opportunity Commission of South Australia**.

- **Website:** [equalopportunity.sa.gov.au](https://www.equalopportunity.sa.gov.au)
- **Phone:** (08) 7322 7070
- **Time Limit:** You must lodge your complaint within **12 months**.

## Key Takeaway

SA provides decent baseline protections, but the broad religious exemptions and lack of vilification laws are significant drawbacks. The legal uncertainty around students in religious schools is particularly concerning and makes SA less protective than states like Victoria or Queensland.
