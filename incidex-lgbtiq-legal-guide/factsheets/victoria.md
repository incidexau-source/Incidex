# Know Your Rights: LGBTIQ+ Discrimination & Vilification (Victoria)

**Last updated: 19 February 2026**

---

## Overall Rating: 🟡 AMBER

Victoria has strong protections for LGBTIQ+ people with comprehensive coverage of sexual orientation, gender identity, and sex characteristics. Religious exemptions were significantly narrowed in 2021, providing much better protection in schools and services. In February 2026, Victoria became the **first Australian state** to pass historic intersex medical safeguards legislation, protecting intersex children from unnecessary medical interventions. However, Victoria **lacks vilification protections** for LGBTIQ+ people despite long-standing promises, which prevents it from being rated Green.

---

## What's Protected

### Sexual Orientation: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against because you are gay, lesbian, bisexual, or any other sexual orientation.

**Real-world examples:**
- Your employer cannot fire you for being gay.
- A landlord cannot refuse to rent to you because you're in a same-sex relationship.
- A hospital cannot deny you treatment based on sexual orientation.
- A café cannot refuse to serve you because you're in a same-sex relationship.

**Limitations:** None. Victoria has had comprehensive protections since 2010.

### Gender Identity: ✅ Protected

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against based on your gender identity, including if you are transgender, non-binary, genderfluid, or agender.

**Real-world examples:**
- Your employer must use your correct name and pronouns.
- You cannot be denied employment because you are transitioning or non-binary.
- A landlord cannot refuse housing because you are trans or non-binary.
- Schools cannot prevent you from using facilities matching your gender identity.
- A religious school cannot refuse to hire you as a teacher just because you're trans.

**Limitations:** None. Victoria updated its definition in September 2025 to be clearly inclusive of non-binary and gender diverse people.

### Sex Characteristics: ✅ Protected + Medical Safeguards (2026)

**What this means:** You cannot be discriminated against based on physical sex characteristics (chromosomes, hormones, anatomy). This includes intersex status. Additionally, intersex children are now protected from unnecessary medical interventions under the **Health Safeguards for People Born with Variations in Sex Characteristics Act 2025**, passed 19 February 2026.

**Real-world examples:**
- You cannot be denied medical care based on being intersex.
- An employer cannot ask invasive questions about your body.
- You cannot be excluded from school activities because of sex characteristics.
- A gym cannot refuse you membership because you are intersex.
- Intersex children cannot be subjected to unnecessary surgeries to alter their sex characteristics without their consent.
- An independent expert panel (with lived experience representatives) must oversee treatment plans for intersex children.

**Limitations:** None. Victoria is a national leader in this protection, having added "sex characteristics" in February 2021 and becoming the **first Australian state** (second jurisdiction after ACT) to legislate intersex medical safeguards in February 2026.

## Where You're Protected

### EMPLOYMENT ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Being rejected for a job because of sexual orientation/gender identity/sex characteristics.
- Being paid less than colleagues because you're LGBTIQ+.
- Being harassed by coworkers or managers.
- Being forced to hide your identity at work.
- Being fired because you transition or come out.
- **Religious schools** refusing to hire you just because you're LGBTIQ+ (they can only discriminate based on religious belief).

**Not protected from:**
- Performance-based discipline (if you're underperforming for legitimate work reasons).

### EDUCATION ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Schools refusing to enroll your child because you're a same-sex couple.
- Universities discriminating in admissions.
- Teachers intentionally using wrong pronouns.
- Being excluded from school events, sports, or facilities.
- **Religious schools** expelling students or refusing enrollment for being LGBTIQ+.

### HOUSING ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Landlords refusing to rent because you're in a same-sex relationship.
- Being evicted because of your gender identity or sex characteristics.

### GOODS & SERVICES ✅
**What you're protected from:**
- Being refused service at a café or restaurant.
- A doctor refusing to treat you.
- **Government-funded religious services** (like aged care or homelessness support) refusing to serve you.

**Not protected from:**
- **Non-government-funded** religious services (they can still discriminate on grounds of sexual orientation and gender identity).

## Exemptions: When Discrimination May Be Legal

### Religious Organisations – NARROW REFORMED EXEMPTIONS

**What this means:** Victoria significantly narrowed religious exemptions in 2021. Religious organisations and schools can no longer discriminate against you just for being LGBTIQ+.

**They CAN:**
- Religious schools can require staff and students to uphold religious beliefs.
- Religious organisations can discriminate in **non-government-funded** service provision (e.g., a private religious retreat).

**They CANNOT:**
- Religious schools **cannot** refuse to hire someone just for being gay or trans.
- Religious schools **cannot** expel students for being LGBTIQ+.
- Government-funded religious services **cannot** discriminate against LGBTIQ+ service users.

**Real example:** A Catholic school can require teachers to uphold Catholic teachings, but cannot refuse to hire a teacher simply because they are gay. The school can only make decisions based on **religious belief**, not LGBTIQ+ status.

**If you face discrimination:** Victoria has some of the strongest protections in Australia. If a religious organisation claims they can discriminate, they must prove it's based on religious belief, not LGBTIQ+ status. Seek advice from VEOHRC.

## Vilification Laws

**Does Victoria have vilification laws for LGBTIQ+ people?**  
❌ NO

Shockingly, Victoria has vilification laws for **race and religion only**. Despite many years of promises from the government, there is still no law in Victoria that makes anti-LGBTIQ+ hate speech or vilification unlawful.

---

## How to Report Discrimination

You can lodge a complaint with the **Victorian Equal Opportunity and Human Rights Commission (VEOHRC)** or go directly to the **Victorian Civil and Administrative Tribunal (VCAT)**.

- **VEOHRC Website:** [humanrights.vic.gov.au](https://www.humanrights.vic.gov.au)
- **VEOHRC Phone:** 1300 292 153
- **VCAT Website:** [vcat.vic.gov.au](https://www.vcat.vic.gov.au)
- **Time Limit:** 12 months from the discrimination.
- **Costs:** No fee for lodging a discrimination complaint at VCAT.

## Key Takeaway

Victoria is a leader in discrimination law, especially with its 2021, 2025, and 2026 reforms. You have very strong protections in schools and workplaces, and the historic 2026 intersex medical safeguards legislation makes Victoria a world leader in protecting intersex children's bodily autonomy. However, the total lack of vilification (hate speech) laws remains a major gap that the community is still fighting to close.
